## Purpose

This knowledge base article explains how to connect to the company VPN from home.

The steps are written for non-technical users who need secure access to company systems, shared drives, or internal applications while working remotely.

---

## When to Use This Guide

Use this guide if:

- you are working from home
- you need access to company systems
- you need access to shared drives or internal applications
- you are connected to home internet but cannot access company resources
- IT Support has asked you to connect to the VPN

---

## Before You Start

Check these basic points first:

- Make sure your home internet is working.
- Make sure your company laptop is turned on and connected to Wi-Fi or Ethernet.
- Make sure you know your company username and password.
- Keep your phone nearby if multi-factor authentication is required.
- Close unnecessary applications if the computer is running slowly.

---

### Step 1 – Check Your Internet Connection

Before connecting to the VPN, confirm that your normal internet connection works.

1. Open a web browser.
2. Try opening a public website, for example:

```text
https://www.google.com
```

If normal websites do not open, fix the internet connection first before trying the VPN.

---

### Step 2 – Open the VPN Application

1. Click Start.
2. Search for the company VPN application.
3. Open the VPN application.

The VPN application name depends on the company setup. Common examples include:

- Cisco AnyConnect
- GlobalProtect
- FortiClient
- OpenVPN
- Windows built-in VPN
- Company-branded VPN tool

---

### Step 3 – Select the Company VPN Connection

1. Select the company VPN profile or connection.
2. Check that the server address is already filled in.
3. Do not change the server address unless IT Support tells you to.

If no VPN profile is visible, contact IT Support.

---

### Step 4 – Sign In

1. Enter your company username.
2. Enter your company password.
3. Click Connect or Sign in.

Use your normal company login details unless IT Support has provided different instructions.

---

### Step 5 – Approve Multi-Factor Authentication

If multi-factor authentication is enabled, approve the login request.

Depending on the company setup, this may be:

- a push notification on your phone
- a code from an authenticator app
- an SMS code
- a phone call confirmation

Only approve the request if you are currently trying to connect to the VPN.

---

### Step 6 – Check That the VPN Is Connected

After signing in, check the VPN status.

The VPN should show a status such as:

- Connected
- VPN connected
- Secure connection established

You may also see a lock symbol or VPN icon near the clock.

---

### Step 7 – Test Company Access

After the VPN is connected, test whether company resources work.

Examples:

- open an internal company website
- open a shared drive
- open a company application
- open Outlook or Teams if required
- access a file location that only works on the company network

If company resources still do not open, disconnect and reconnect the VPN once.

---

### Step 8 – Disconnect the VPN When Finished

When you no longer need access to company systems:

1. Open the VPN application.
2. Click Disconnect.
3. Confirm that the VPN status changes to disconnected.

Disconnecting the VPN when it is not needed can help avoid connection issues and unnecessary network traffic.

---

### Troubleshooting

#### The VPN does not connect

Try these steps:

- Check that your home internet works.
- Restart the VPN application.
- Check your username and password.
- Restart the computer.
- Try connecting again.

---

#### The password is not accepted

Check:

- whether Caps Lock is on
- whether the username is typed correctly
- whether your password has recently changed
- whether your account may be locked

Contact IT Support if the password still does not work.

---

#### Multi-factor authentication does not appear

Check:

- whether your phone has internet access
- whether the authenticator app is working
- whether notifications are enabled
- whether the time on your phone is correct

Contact IT Support if you cannot approve the login.

---

#### VPN is connected, but company resources do not open

Try these steps:

- Disconnect and reconnect the VPN.
- Restart the browser or application.
- Check whether the shared drive or application works for other users.
- Restart the computer.
- Contact IT Support if the issue continues.

---

### When to Contact IT Support

Contact IT Support if:

- the VPN application is missing
- no VPN profile is visible
- your login does not work
- your account is locked
- multi-factor authentication does not work
- the VPN connects but company systems are still not accessible
- you receive an error message you do not understand

---

### Information to Give IT Support

When contacting IT Support, include:

- your device name, if known
- the VPN application name
- whether your home internet works
- whether you can log in
- whether multi-factor authentication appears
- the exact error message, if shown
- when the problem started
- steps you already tried

Example message:

```text
Hello IT Support,

I cannot connect to the company VPN from home. My home internet is working, but the VPN does not connect.

I already restarted the VPN application and restarted the computer. The error message says: [insert error message].

Thank you.
```

---

## Security Notes

- Do not share your company password with anyone.
- Only approve multi-factor authentication requests that you started yourself.
- Use only the official company VPN application.
- Do not change VPN settings unless IT Support tells you to.
- Disconnect the VPN when you no longer need company access.

---

## Summary

To connect to the company VPN from home, first check your internet connection, open the VPN application, select the company VPN profile, sign in with your company account, approve multi-factor authentication, and test access to company resources.

If the VPN does not work, contact IT Support and explain what you already checked.
