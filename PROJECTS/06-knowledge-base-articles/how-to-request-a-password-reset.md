## Purpose

This knowledge base article explains what to do if you cannot sign in and need to request a password reset.

The goal is to help users contact IT Support with the correct information and avoid unsafe password handling.

---

## When to Use This Guide

Use this guide if:

- You forgot your password.
- Your password is not accepted.
- Your account may be locked.
- You recently changed your password and cannot sign in.
- You cannot access Windows, VPN, Outlook, Teams, or shared drives because of a password issue.

---

## Before You Contact IT Support

Check these basic points first:

- Make sure **Caps Lock** is not turned on.
- Check that you are typing the correct username.
- Check that you are using your company account, not a personal account.
- Try signing in once more carefully.
- Do not keep trying many times if the password is rejected, because this may lock the account.

---

## What Not to Do

For security reasons:

- Do not send your password by email, chat, or ticket.
- Do not tell anyone your current password.
- Do not write your password in screenshots or notes.
- Do not use someone else’s account.
- Do not approve unexpected MFA requests.
- Do not ask another employee to reset your password unless they are authorized IT Support.

---

## Step 1 — Contact IT Support

Contact IT Support using the approved company support channel.

This may be:

- Support ticket
- IT Support email
- Service desk portal
- Phone support
- Company chat support, if approved

Use only official company support channels.

---

## Step 2 — Provide the Required Information

When contacting IT Support, include:

- Your full name
- Your username or company email address
- The device you are using
- Whether you are onsite or working remotely
- Which service you cannot access
- When the issue started
- Any error message shown
- Whether you recently changed your password
- Whether MFA appears or not

Do not include your password.

---

## Step 3 — Verify Your Identity

IT Support may ask you to verify your identity before resetting the password.

Depending on company policy, this may include:

- Employee ID
- Callback to a registered phone number
- Manager confirmation
- Security questions, if approved
- Verification through the company service desk process

A password reset should not be completed until your identity is verified.

---

## Step 4 — Follow IT Support Instructions

After your identity is verified, IT Support may provide a temporary password or direct you to a company password reset portal.

Follow the instructions carefully.

If you receive a temporary password:

1. Sign in with the temporary password.
2. Change the password when prompted.
3. Choose a new password that follows company password rules.
4. Do not reuse old passwords.
5. Do not share the new password with anyone.

---

## Step 5 — Update Saved Passwords

After changing your password, you may need to update saved passwords in other places.

Check:

- Outlook
- Teams
- OneDrive
- VPN
- Mobile email app
- Mapped network drives
- Windows Credential Manager, if instructed by IT Support

If your account locks again after the password reset, tell IT Support immediately.

---

## If You Work Remotely

If you work from home, password changes may need extra steps.

You may need to:

- Connect to VPN
- Use the company password reset portal
- Lock and unlock Windows after connecting to VPN
- Restart the computer
- Update saved credentials in Microsoft 365 apps

Follow the exact instructions from IT Support.

---

## When to Contact IT Support Again

Contact IT Support again if:

- The temporary password does not work.
- You cannot change the password.
- Your account locks again.
- MFA does not work.
- You cannot connect to VPN after the password change.
- Outlook, Teams, or OneDrive keeps asking for the old password.
- You see an error message you do not understand.

---

## Example Message to IT Support

```text
Hello IT Support,

I cannot sign in to my company account and I need a password reset.

My username/email is: [insert username or email]
I am working: [onsite / remotely]
The affected service is: [Windows / VPN / Outlook / Teams / OneDrive / other]
The issue started: [insert date/time]
Error message: [insert error message, if any]

I have not shared my password and I can verify my identity if needed.

Thank you.
```

---

## Security Notes

- IT Support should never ask for your current password.
- Never send passwords in plain text unless your company has a specific approved temporary-password process.
- Only use official company support channels.
- Report suspicious login prompts or unexpected MFA requests.
- Change temporary passwords as soon as instructed.
- Keep your new password private.

---

## Summary

If you need a password reset, contact IT Support through the approved support channel, provide the required account and device information, and verify your identity.

Never share your current password, and do not include passwords in tickets, emails, chats, screenshots, or notes.
