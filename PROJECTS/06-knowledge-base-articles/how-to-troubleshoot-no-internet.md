## Purpose

This knowledge base article explains simple steps a user can follow when the computer is connected to Wi-Fi or Ethernet, but the internet is not working.

The steps are written for non-technical users and should be tried before contacting IT support.

---

## When to Use This Guide

Use this guide if:

- websites do not load
- Microsoft Teams, Outlook, or other online services do not connect
- the Wi-Fi icon shows a problem
- the computer says “No Internet”
- the internet works on another device, but not on this computer

---

## Before You Start

Check these basic points first:

- Make sure the Wi-Fi is turned on.
- If you use an Ethernet cable, check that the cable is plugged in properly.
- Check if other devices have internet access.
- If you are at home, check whether the router is turned on.
- If you are in the office, check whether other users have the same issue.

---

## Step 1 – Check the Network Icon

Look at the network icon in the bottom-right corner of the screen.

Possible signs of a problem:

- globe icon
- warning symbol
- “No Internet”
- “Not connected”

If Wi-Fi is disconnected, click the network icon and reconnect to the correct Wi-Fi network.

---

## Step 2 – Turn Wi-Fi Off and On Again

1. Click the network icon in the bottom-right corner.
2. Turn Wi-Fi off.
3. Wait 10 seconds.
4. Turn Wi-Fi on again.
5. Reconnect to the correct Wi-Fi network.

Then try opening a website again.

---
